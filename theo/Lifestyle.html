Fashion

1. MONO Fashion

MONO is a modern Hungarian fashion store located downtown in the 5th district, not far from the Astoria metro stop, serving men, women, and sometimes babies. Stop at MONO for minimalist, but eye catching designs by local designers. Their sister shop MONO Art and Design is just a little further up the street.

2. Dorko (DRK)

Dorko, although an international brand, is designed by an ecelctic array of Hungarian artists,
designers, musicians, and tattoo artists. Their flagship store is located on Andrassy Avenue,
near the Opera House and not far from the Basilica. Stop by DRK for some truly creative sneakers,
bags, and accessories for men and women.

3. Nubu & Nude by Nubu

Both located in the popular shopping area, Andrassy Avenue, Nubu & Nude by Nubu are run by the same owners as the MONO stores. Nubu certainly echoes their minimalist style. Stop by Nubu for clean silhouettes with stunning fabrics inspired by the fine arts.

4. Punch

Just off of Andrassy Avenue, Punch is a concept store for women’s clothing, accessories, and jewelry owned and operated by designers. At Punch, you’ll find yourself discussing your favorite piece with the
designer who created it. Don’t miss Punch for unique patters, feminine jewelry, and handmade leather goods.

5. The Garden Studio

The Garden Studio is an adorable, feminine design store near Andrassy Avenue, on the same street as Punch, so be sure to check them out at the same time. If you are planning on visiting the Opera, definitely drop by The Garden Studio for striking colors, vintage influences, and whimsical patterns while you’re in the area

6. Eva's Room

Eva’s Room is a fashion showroom beautifully decorated like an intimate sitting room. Eva’s goal was to bring you a relaxing shopping experience free of the crowds at the mall. The showroom is open Tuesday through Friday or anytime by appointment. Eva’s Room mostly features women’s clothing, leather goods, jewelry, and eyewear.

7. Hababa Designer Store and Gallery

A relatively new addition to the 5th district, not far from Deak Ferenc Ter, Hababa Design Store sells a selection of Hungarian art, women’s and vintage clothing, and accessories. The store is designed so beautifully that it’s worth a stop for its sake alone. Travelers love Hababa’s unique jewelry, reasonable prices, and truly rare finds.

8. Lollipop Factory

Lollipop Factory is both a fashion store and exhibition space for local artists, on Kiraly Utca, a popular area for bars and shopping. It’s just a few minute’s walk from both the Opera House and the Great Synagogue, so a stop here can be a great addition to your sightseeing itinerary. Lollipop Factory offers limited edition, sustainably made clothing for men and women.

9. Romani

Possibly the most colorful on the list, Romani is a local design label inspired by traditional Roma culture. Erika Varga, the founder, believes fashion can act as a bridge builder between cultures. The design studio also provides career and educational opportunities to women in need. Erika’s work has been internationally recognized; in 2012 she was awarded the “Glamour Women of the Year Award.”
